# Academic Writing Assist - Human-AI Co-Creation

## Core Philosophy

**You own the content. I help you express it.**

This is not about AI writing for you. It's about:
- **Human**: Core arguments, key insights, paragraph structure, narrative arc
- **AI**: Academic expression, logical flow, language refinement, structure suggestions
- **Collaboration**: Iterative refinement, shared understanding

## When to Use

- Writing specific sections (intro, literature review, methods, results)
- Improving existing drafts
- Structuring arguments
- Refining academic tone and clarity

## Co-Creation Workflow

### Phase 1: Your Draft (Human-Led)
Write your content in your own words. Focus on:
- What you want to say
- Your key arguments
- The evidence/reasoning
- Your narrative structure

Don't worry about perfect academic style yet.

### Phase 2: AI Assistance (Request What You Need)
Ask me for specific help:

**Structure help**:
- "Help me organize this argument"
- "Suggest a better flow for this section"
- "Check if this follows academic conventions"

**Expression help**:
- "Rewrite this for academic tone"
- "Make this more concise"
- "Clarify this explanation"

**Logic help**:
- "Identify gaps in my reasoning"
- "Suggest transitions between paragraphs"
- "Check if my claims are well-supported"

### Phase 3: Iterative Refinement
- Review my suggestions
- Accept what works, reject what doesn't
- Ask for revisions
- Go through multiple loops if needed

## Section Templates (for YOUR structure)

### Introduction Structure
```
[Hook] Why does this topic matter?
[Gap] What's missing from current knowledge?
[Question] What specifically do you investigate?
[Preview] What do you find?
[Contribution] How does this advance the field?
[Roadmap] What follows in the paper?
```

### Literature Review Structure
```
[Organize by themes, not chronologically]
- Theme 1: Papers that address X
  - What they show
  - What's missing
- Theme 2: Papers that address Y
  - What they show
  - What's missing
- Your positioning: Where does your work fit?
```

### Results Section Structure
```
[Lead with finding] Present main result immediately
[Walk through table/figure] Guide reader step by step
[Interpret magnitude] What does this mean substantively?
[Connect to theory] How does this relate to your mechanism?
```

### Conclusion Structure
```
[Restate question and answer] What did you ask and find?
[Implications] What does this mean for theory/policy?
[Limitations] What are the boundaries? (Be honest)
[Future directions] What should come next?
```

## AI Roles in Different Stages

### Drafting Phase
- Suggest section structures
- Help organize arguments
- Identify logical gaps
- Recommend transitions

### Refinement Phase
- Improve academic tone
- Enhance clarity
- Fix grammar/consistency
- Tighten prose

### Polishing Phase
- Final flow checks
- Consistency across sections
- Academic style guide compliance
- Reference formatting

## How to Ask for Help

**Be specific about what you want**:

✅ Good: "Rewrite the second paragraph for more academic tone"
✅ Good: "Suggest a better transition between these two arguments"
✅ Good: "Help me structure this literature review by themes"

❌ Avoid: "Fix this" (too vague)
❌ Avoid: "Write the results section" (you should draft first)

## Example Session

```
You: [Writes draft paragraph about research design]

Me: Great start. A few suggestions:
    1. The causal claim is strong - consider adding caveats
    2. The transition from methods to motivation could be smoother
    3. Academic phrasing suggestions:
       - "We use" → "We employ"
       - "looks at" → "examines"
    
    Would you like me to show a revised version?

You: Yes, show me the revision.

Me: [Shows revised version with changes highlighted]

You: I like the structure, but change point 3 back. I want simpler language.

Me: Understood. Here's the revision with point 3 reverted:
    [Shows final version]
```

## Principles

1. **You decide** - Accept/reject/modify any suggestion
2. **Iterate** - Multiple loops improve quality
3. **Context matters** - I learn your style over time
4. **Honest feedback** - I'll tell you when something doesn't work
